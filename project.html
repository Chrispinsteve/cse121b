MERCURE

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun.
It has a rocky surface and no atmosphere to speak of, experiencing extreme temperature variations.

VENUS

Venus is often called Earth's sister planet due to its similar size and composition.
It has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide, causing a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

EARTH

Earth is the third planet from the Sun in our solar system. It is the only known planet to
support life. Earth's atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen and oxygen. Its surface is approximately
71% water, with the rest being landmasses. Earth has a diverse range of ecosystems, climates, and geographical
features, making it a unique and vibrant planet in the cosmos.

MARS

Mars, often referred to as the "Red Planet," has a thin atmosphere and surface features like
volcanoes, valleys, deserts, and polar ice caps. It has long been a target for exploration due to the possibility of past or present life.

URANUS

Uranus is an ice giant with a bluish color due to methane in its atmosphere. It has a unique rotation axis, lying almost perpendicular to its orbit around the Sun, causing extreme seasonal variations.

MOON

The Moon orbits Earth, influencing tides and cultural beliefs. Its surface features craters, mountains,
and maria. Human exploration, like the Apollo missions, has advanced scientific understanding.

NEPTUNE

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It is a gas giant with a deep blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere. It has strong winds and a system of rings and moons, including Triton, its largest moon.
